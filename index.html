The short version

Who's building this

I'm a junior electrical engineering student at UConn, splitting my time between two research labs, a teaching assistantship, and a workshop where I keep trying to build semiconductor fabrication equipment out of 3D-printer parts.

I care about specific numbers, honest tradeoffs, and projects that end in a device you can actually measure. If something gets hand-waved, I'm the person who asks for the plot.

For the record 6'1", curly auburn hair, glasses, punk-femme, and strong opinions on coupling regimes. Builder first, asker-of-annoying-questions a close second.
Two labs

In the lab

Photons in one room, thin films in the other.

Prof. Wang · silicon photonics

Microrings that resonate

Microring and microdisk resonators for dense wavelength-division multiplexing. I implemented the Novick transfer-matrix model straight from the paper in MATLAB, came up to speed on Bogaerts as a sophomore, and — early on — caught the wrong simulation tool being pointed at the problem.

Prof. Gokirmak · optics & thin films

Measuring films by their color

I built a reflectance-spectroscopy rig to measure SiO₂ thickness on silicon with an OceanOptics HR2000+, then ran it live with the professor watching. What made it work: normalizing against bare silicon rather than an oxide reference, and killing saturation with integration time instead of dimming the tungsten-halogen source. Report in progress.
